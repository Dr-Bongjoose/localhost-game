# LOCALHOST - Social & Viral Features

## Social Features (Lightweight, Launch-appropriate)

### Leaderboards

| Category | Reset |
|----------|-------|
| Total data extracted (all-time) | Never |
| Most unique strains bred | Never |
| Most zones controlled | Never |
| Most legendary/mythic strains | Never |
| Weekly data extracted | Every week |

Simple competitive features that drive engagement without needing real-time multiplayer.

### Codex Sharing

- Share your strain discoveries via a generated image (strain visual + traits + name)
- This is the viral loop. Every shared strain is free marketing.
- "Look at this ridiculous organism I bred" is a natural social media post
- Generated images should be dark, striking, and immediately recognizable as LOCALHOST

### Weekly Challenges

| Example Challenge | Reward |
|-------------------|--------|
| "Breed a strain with stealth above X" | Currency + codex credit |
| "Occupy a government zone for 24 hours" | Currency + cosmetic |
| "Breed 5 strains in one day" | Mutation attempt |

These give players goals beyond open-ended progression.

## Post-Launch Social (Phase 4)

### Guilds / Syndicates

- Players pool resources for shared boosts
- Cooperative breeding (two players each contribute a parent)
- Guild leaderboards
- Guild-exclusive strains

## Viral Strategy

1. The discovery moment is the core viral mechanic. Every new strain bred is a shareable moment.
2. Dark biological aesthetic stands out in feeds full of bright colorful games.
3. "Malware achieved sentience and reproduces like living organisms" is a pitch that makes people curious.
4. The name LOCALHOST is recognizable and slightly unsettling in context.
5. Procedurally generated strain names and descriptions create unique shareable content ("I bred a 'Necrotic Threadworm' with legendary stealth").
6. Codex completion percentage gives players a reason to share progress.
